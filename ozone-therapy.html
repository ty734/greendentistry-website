Medical-Grade Ozone Therapy · Alexandria, VA

A Gentler Way to Reduce Bacteria and Support Healing

Your body is remarkably good at healing when we give it the right support. At Green Dentistry, Dr. Park uses medical-grade ozone as part of a biological approach designed to reduce harmful bacteria and help your body recover comfortably after treatment.

What It Is

What Is Ozone Therapy?

Ozone sounds high-tech, but it is simply oxygen in an activated form. Think of it as oxygen with a little extra energy, the same gentle helper your body already relies on every day.

In dentistry, medical-grade ozone has been used for many years as a natural way to disinfect. We can deliver it as a gas, in ozonated water, or in an oil, depending on what each situation calls for.

Because it works with your body rather than against it, ozone has become one of the most versatile tools in biological dentistry. It lets us care for your mouth thoughtfully, with fewer chemicals along the way.

How We Use It

How Ozone Fits Into Your Care

Ozone is rarely the whole treatment. More often, it is the quiet step that makes the rest of your care gentler and more thorough. Dr. Park may use it in several ways:

Disinfecting before restorations. Cleaning a tooth with ozone before placing a filling is designed to reduce the bacteria left behind, supporting a healthier foundation for the restoration.

Supporting gum health. Ozonated water can be used to flush gum pockets and help reduce the bacteria associated with inflammation and bleeding gums.

Comfortable healing. After an extraction or other procedure, ozone may be used to disinfect the area and support your body's natural recovery.

Is It Right for You?

You Might Benefit From Ozone If You…

Many patients are a good fit for ozone-supported care. It may be worth a conversation if you:

•  Prefer a natural, holistic approach to your dental care
•  Live with gum inflammation, bleeding, or sensitivity
•  Are recovering from a procedure or infection
•  Would rather lean on gentler options than chemicals when possible
•  Care about how your oral health connects to your whole body

Even if ozone therapy is new to you, it may be a helpful piece of a more thoughtful, whole-body plan. The best place to start is a comprehensive new patient exam, where Dr. Park can talk through what makes sense for you.
