Breathe Easy: Understanding Airway-Centered Dentistry

By Dr. Hye Park, DMD · Published September 18, 2024

Breathing is fundamental to life, yet millions of people experience airway insufficiencies that impact their ability to breathe well. At Green Dentistry, we understand the vital role of proper breathing in your overall health and well-being. Enter Airway-Centered Dentistry, a meaningful approach to addressing disordered breathing and improving quality of life.

The Impact of Disordered Breathing

Did you know that disordered breathing, often most noticeable during sleep, affects millions of American adults? Many cases go undiagnosed. When left unaddressed, disordered breathing can be associated with a host of symptoms and health issues tied to inadequate oxygen supply, including sleep apnea, chronic fatigue, and behavioral problems.

Our Innovative Approach

Dr. Hye Park at Green Dentistry is committed to your oral health and overall physical well-being. With specialized training in sleep and airway dentistry, Dr. Park practices Airway-Centered Dentistry to address various concerns affecting patients of all ages. This field focuses on understanding how we breathe and the factors contributing to common sleep problems. Dr. Park's training allows her to help identify and address the underlying causes of sleep issues, supporting more comprehensive care.

What We Treat

At Green Dentistry, we use an airway-centered approach to address a range of oral and overall health concerns tied to sleep issues. Here's a look at what we offer:

  • Biomimetic Guides & Appliances: These devices help correct structural issues that can contribute to disordered breathing.
  • Oral Appliances: An effective alternative to CPAP for obstructive sleep apnea and upper airway resistance syndrome in adults.
  • Laser Treatment for Snoring: Non-invasive laser therapy that may help reduce snoring and improve sleep quality.

For children, our approach aims to support healthy development and may reduce the need for certain treatments related to sleep disturbances. By addressing these issues early, we help pave the way for healthier development and better overall health.

Benefits of Functional Breathing

Functional breathing offers a range of benefits, from improved sleep and mood to enhanced dental health and overall well-being. Patients often experience reduced snoring, better posture, and increased energy and focus. By addressing airway issues, we help you work toward not only better dental health but also a higher quality of life.

Are You Experiencing Airway Issues?

If you experience symptoms such as teeth clenching, snoring, chronic headaches, or difficulty sleeping, you may have an airway issue. Airway-Centered Dentistry could be a key to a healthier, more restful life. At Green Dentistry, we are dedicated to helping you breathe easier and live better.

Take the First Step Towards Better Breathing

Ready to explore the benefits of Airway-Centered Dentistry? Contact Green Dentistry today to schedule a consultation with Dr. Hye Park. Together, we'll work to uncover the root causes of your breathing issues and create a personalized plan designed to support your health and well-being.

Don't let disordered breathing impact your quality of life. Reach out to us and take the first step towards better breathing and a brighter, healthier future!
